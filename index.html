Meet the Therapist

Southern Roots.
Grounded Healing.

"Collaboration, respect for each individual's knowledge about their life, and practical steps toward greater well-being."

A Southern girl from New Orleans, Louisiana, Trenese Johnson brings warmth, depth, and 28 years of professional social work experience to every session. As a U.S. Army Veteran who served during Desert Storm in 1991, she understands firsthand the complexity of navigating stress, identity, and healing across multiple chapters of life.

Licensed as a Clinical Social Worker since 2000 and holding her MSW from Southern University at New Orleans, Trenese has built a practice rooted in affirming, culturally sensitive care — particularly for women, the LGBTQ+ community, veterans, and those navigating sexual wellness and identity. She sees clients in person in Atlanta, GA and online throughout Georgia and Washington, D.C.

How I Work

Evidence-Based Care
with a Whole-Body Lens.

"Utilizing relaxation techniques and meditative or breathing exercises, along with traditional talk therapy — because healing happens in the mind and the body."
◎

Somatic Therapy

Body-centered practices including breathwork, relaxation techniques, and mindful movement to release stored stress and trauma.

⬡

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Evidence-based techniques that identify and shift thought patterns fueling anxiety, depression, and unhelpful behaviors.

✦

Solution-Focused Therapy

Brief, practical conversations centered on your strengths and where you want to go — not only what's gone wrong.

∞

Mindfulness-Based Practice

Present-moment awareness woven throughout sessions to build resilience, regulate the nervous system, and reconnect with self.
